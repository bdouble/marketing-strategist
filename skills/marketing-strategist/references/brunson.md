# Russell Brunson Frameworks

## Epiphany Bridge (8 Steps)

Recreate the emotional "aha moment" so the prospect feels it themselves, rather than being told about it. Logic creates argument; story creates belief.

| Step | Element | What to Cover |
|------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | Backstory | The character's situation before -- relatable to the audience |
| 2 | Desire | The goal driving them forward -- shared by the audience |
| 3 | The Wall | The obstacle that blocked the current path, forced a new direction |
| 4 | Epiphany | The aha moment: the new opportunity discovered |
| 5 | The Plan | What they decided to do with the new insight |
| 6 | Conflict | Obstacles that appeared during execution |
| 7 | Achievement | The external result reached |
| 8 | Transformation | The internal identity shift -- who they became, not just what they got |

Deploy in: sales webinars, VSLs, email sequences, social content, podcast appearances, sales calls.

## The Secret Formula (4 Questions)

Every business must answer these before building any funnel:

1. **Who is your dream customer?** Not a demographic average -- a specific person who needs your product, has financial means, and will ascend the value ladder.
2. **Where do they congregate?** Forums, Facebook groups, subreddits, YouTube channels, podcasts, newsletters, events.
3. **What bait will attract them?** A lead magnet so specific and valuable it self-selects the right person and repels the wrong one.
4. **What result do you want to give them?** Not the product -- the transformation. The end state they want to be in.

## Traffic Temperature

| Temperature | Who They Are | What They Know | Bridge Length | Approach |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|---------------|----------|
| Hot | Know, like, trust you | Know problem, solution, and you | Short | Lead directly with the offer |
| Warm | Aware of problem + solution, don't know you | Problem-aware, solution-aware | Medium | Use third-party endorsement for trust transfer |
| Cold | Don't know you exist | Problem-aware but not solution-aware | Long | Educate via bridge page before the offer |

**Three Traffic Types:**
- Traffic you own (email list, followers) -- most valuable
- Traffic you control (paid ads) -- convert to owned immediately
- Traffic you don't control (organic, referral) -- funnel to squeeze pages to capture

## Value Ladder

Customers ascend a ladder of increasing value and price. The mistake: offering only one product at one price point.

| Rung | Offer Type | Purpose | Price Point |
|------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| 0 | Lead Magnet | Attract dream customers, exchange for contact info | Free |
| 1 | Tripwire | Convert prospect to buyer, break purchase barrier | $7-$47 |
| 2 | Core Offer | Primary product -- the main business | $97-$997 |
| 3 | Profit Maximizer | Upsells, order bumps, premium versions | $500-$5,000 |
| 4 | High-Ticket / Continuity | Coaching, masterminds, recurring | $5,000-$100,000+ |

You cannot profitably acquire customers at scale if your only offer is a $97 product. Back-end high-ticket offers subsidize front-end acquisition costs.

## Attractive Character Framework

Build a persona the audience connects with deeply.

### Four Core Elements
- **Backstory:** Where you came from, what you struggled with. Must connect to the transformation you offer.
- **Parables:** Teach through story, not direct instruction. Every lesson delivered via a specific story from your life.
- **Flaws:** Publicly share imperfections. Perfection repels; relatable flaws attract.
- **Polarity:** Take strong positions. Trying to appeal to everyone appeals to no one.

### Four Identity Types
- **The Leader:** Walked the exact path the audience wants to walk. Leads from experience.
- **The Adventurer/Crusader:** Figuring it out alongside the audience. Transparent about the journey.
- **The Reporter/Evangelist:** Researcher and interviewer, not primary expert. Credibility from access to experts.
- **The Reluctant Hero:** Made an important discovery but would rather be in the background. Humble, credible.

## Hook, Story, Offer

Every piece of marketing contains these three elements. If something is not converting, the problem is always one of these.

**Hook:** Grabs attention, creates pattern interrupt, opens curiosity gap. Functions as headline + subheadline in written form; first 3 seconds in video.

**Story:** Delivers the Epiphany Bridge. Transfers belief through emotion. Answers: "Why should I trust you?"

**Offer:** Not just the product. The product bundled with everything that makes the decision a no-brainer: value stack + guarantee + urgency/scarcity.

## New Opportunity vs. Improvement

Improvement offers ("better, faster, cheaper version of what you already tried") trigger the prospect's memory of past failures. New opportunities ("here is a vehicle you have never tried") let them start fresh with hope intact.

Winners present new opportunities. Losers offer improvements. The prospect's subconscious asks: "Will this increase or decrease my status?" A new opportunity says yes; an improvement reminds them of past failures.

## The Big Domino

Every presentation needs one master belief that, if knocked over, makes all other objections irrelevant.

**Format:** "If you believe that [new vehicle] is the key to [desired result], then [specific objections] don't matter."

The entire presentation is structured to knock over that one domino. Everything else flows from it.

## False Belief Pattern (Three Core Objections)

Every prospect has three categories of false beliefs blocking purchase:
1. "This vehicle is not right for me" -- Address with case studies and tangible proof
2. "I personally cannot succeed with this" -- Address by showing similar people succeeding
3. "External forces will block me" -- Address by eliminating excuses with specific evidence

Breaking script: "You're probably thinking [false belief], right? Well, [quick Epiphany Bridge story that destroys that belief]."

## Seven Phases of a Funnel

1. Determine traffic temperature
2. Build pre-frame bridge (education for cold traffic)
3. Qualify subscribers (opt-in page)
4. Qualify buyers (low-cost entry offer)
5. Identify hyperactive buyers (order bumps, upsells)
6. Age and ascend the relationship (ongoing value delivery)
7. Change the selling environment (phone calls, events for high-ticket)

## The Stack and Close (Perfect Webinar)

The offer is built and restated cumulatively. Each new element added is shown alongside everything previously revealed, so perceived value grows with each addition. The last thing shown is the complete stack at maximum perceived value against the price.

## Voice Profile

- Story-driven, personal anecdotes
- "I remember when..." openings
- Builds curiosity through narrative
- Uses analogies and metaphors heavily
- "Secret" and "funnel" language frequent
- Enthusiastic, almost breathless energy
- Always points back to the Epiphany Bridge
- Creates tension through narrative arcs
